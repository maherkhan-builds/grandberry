# Grandberry — Devpost Demo Video Script

**Target video length:** 2 minutes 58 seconds  
**Estimated spoken narration:** approximately 2 minutes 42 seconds  
**Live demo:** https://grandberry.vercel.app  
**Recommended browser:** Chrome with microphone permission set to Ask

## Before recording

- Open the live demo in Chrome and close unrelated tabs or notifications.
- Confirm the microphone works, but reset the site permission to **Ask** before the final take.
- Use in-app links when changing roles so temporary notes and audio remain available.
- Prepare one short spoken Memory Moment, such as: “Every Sunday, our family made breakfast together and told stories around the table.”
- Keep the cursor still while speaking and move it only when the next action is described.
- Do not refresh after creating temporary notes, replies, or memories.

---

## 0:00–0:20 — Introduce Grandberry

**Maximum duration:** 20 seconds  
**Page or route:** `/` — `https://grandberry.vercel.app/`

### Exact narration

> “Grandberry is a voice-first companion for older adults and their families. It turns a simple morning routine into gentle connection—helping a senior check in, helping family stay informed without clinical language, and keeping everyday love and family stories close.”

### Actions

1. Begin on the Grandberry role-selection page.
2. Slowly point to **Continue as Senior** and **Continue as Family**.
3. Click **Continue as Senior** at approximately 0:18.

### Visible on screen

- Grandberry name and “Love, close at hand.”
- The Williams Family demo-household label.
- Both role-selection cards.

### Recording notes

- Start with the full landing page visible.
- Speak warmly and avoid rushing the first sentence.
- Do not describe Grandberry as medical care or monitoring.

## 0:20–0:55 — Senior morning check-in

**Maximum duration:** 35 seconds  
**Pages or routes:** `/senior`, then `/senior/check-in`

### Exact narration

> “Margaret’s senior view is intentionally calm: large text, high contrast, and one clear action at a time. The morning check-in asks six structured questions about sleep, mood, comfort, water, a first meal, and medication confirmation. Progress is always visible, answers can be changed before finishing, and the language stays simple and respectful.”

### Actions

1. On `/senior`, click **Start my morning check-in**.
2. On the Sleep question, choose **Pretty well**, then click **Next**.
3. On Mood, choose **Good**, then click **Next**.
4. Quickly show the progress indicator and scroll no further than necessary.
5. Use prepared selections to move through the remaining questions.
6. On the review screen, point briefly to a **Change** button.

### Visible on screen

- “Good morning, Margaret.”
- Large answer cards.
- “Question 1 of 6” and the progress bar.
- Back and Next controls.
- The review screen if timing permits.

### Recording notes

- Pre-practice the six selections so this section remains under 35 seconds.
- A speed-up edit is acceptable for repeated clicks, but keep the interface readable.
- Do not imply that answers are medically interpreted.

## 0:55–1:25 — Family dashboard

**Maximum duration:** 30 seconds  
**Page or route:** `/family`

### Exact narration

> “The family dashboard gives Margaret’s family gentle context: today’s seeded check-in status, reported or confirmed answers, meaningful activity, and a seven-day pattern. If a usual check-in is missed, Silence is a Signal suggests a caring follow-up—but clearly says Margaret may simply be busy or away. Grandberry provides connection, not medical advice, diagnosis, emergency monitoring, or guaranteed alerts.”

### Actions

1. Use **Choose another role**.
2. Click **Continue as Family**.
3. In **Demo today’s status**, click **Completed**, then **Missed**.
4. Point to the **Silence is a Signal** card.
5. Click **I’ll follow up**.
6. Briefly show the reported answers and seven-day pattern.

### Visible on screen

- Margaret Williams and the Family view label.
- Completed, Not yet completed, and Missed demo controls.
- The gentle missed-check-in wording.
- The safety boundary near the bottom of the dashboard.

### Recording notes

- Say “seeded” so judges understand this is fictional demonstration data.
- Keep the safety boundary readable for at least one second.

## 1:25–1:55 — Family Corner connection

**Maximum duration:** 30 seconds  
**Pages or routes:** `/family`, then `/senior`

### Exact narration

> “Family Corner makes connection feel personal. Maya can send Margaret a short love note exactly as written. In the senior view, Margaret can read it in large type, ask the browser to read it aloud, or record a simple voice reply. Audio stays in temporary browser memory for this demo and is not sent to an external service.”

### Actions

2. Click **Send love note**.
3. Use **Choose another role**, then **Continue as Senior**.
4. Scroll to Family Corner.
5. Point to the received note and click **Read it to me**.
6. Briefly point to **Start recording** without beginning a voice reply.

### Visible on screen

- Character counter and send confirmation.
- “Maya sent you a note.”
- The exact note in large text.
- Read-aloud and voice-reply controls.

### Recording notes

- Use in-app navigation; do not reload or type a new route.
- Lower computer volume if browser speech would compete with narration.

## 1:55–2:25 — Memory Moment and Family Memories

**Maximum duration:** 30 seconds  
**Pages or routes:** `/senior`, then `/family`

### Exact narration

> “Memory Moment invites Margaret to answer one meaningful prompt in her own voice. Recording begins only after permission, stops automatically at ninety seconds, and can be played or replaced before it is added. I’ll add this short story to the temporary family collection, then switch roles so the family can hear Margaret’s memory alongside a clearly labeled demo example with no fabricated audio.”

### Actions

1. On `/senior`, scroll to **Memory Moment**.
2. Click **Start my story** and click **Allow** in Chrome if prompted.
3. Speak the prepared one-sentence story.
4. Click **Stop recording**.
5. Briefly show **Play my story** and **Record again**.
6. Click **Add to family memories**.
7. Show the confirmation message.
8. Use **Choose another role**, then **Continue as Family**.
9. Scroll to **Family Memories** and play the new Shared memory briefly.

### Visible on screen

- Exact family-tradition prompt and privacy message.
- Recording indicator, timer, and Stop recording button.
- Playback and replacement controls.
- Temporary-collection confirmation.
- Shared memory with Margaret, timestamp, and audio player.
- Metadata-only **Demo example** with no audio player.

### Recording notes

- Keep the recorded story to four or five seconds.
- The browser permission prompt may not appear if permission was remembered.
- Do not refresh while moving between roles.
